Why Use This Tool?

Government portals for passport and visa applications often have strict file size and format requirements. A US passport photo upload needs to be under 240KB in JPEG format. Schengen visa portals may require photos under 500KB. Indian passport applications need specific pixel dimensions.

Most free online converters upload your files to their servers — meaning your passport photo, ID scan, or personal documents pass through unknown infrastructure. This tool processes everything inside your browser using the Canvas API. Nothing leaves your device.

Use the country presets to instantly resize and compress your photo to meet exact government specifications. Or use the manual controls for custom dimensions and target file sizes.

How to Reduce Photo Size to 200KB

Many government portals require photos under a specific file size — 200KB, 240KB, or 500KB. Here's how to reduce your photo size without losing quality:

  1. Upload your photo using the drop zone above
  2. Click the Compress tab
  3. Enter your target size (e.g. 200 KB)
  4. Click "Compress File" — the tool uses binary search to find the best quality at your target size
  5. Download the result

This works for any target: reduce photo to 100KB, 200KB, 240KB, 500KB, or any custom size. The algorithm preserves maximum quality while hitting your exact file size limit.

Convert JPG to PNG, PNG to JPG, WebP to JPG

Some government portals only accept JPEG files. Others require PNG. This tool converts between all common image formats:

  • JPG to PNG — when you need transparency or lossless quality
  • PNG to JPG — when the portal requires JPEG format (most common for visa applications)
  • WebP to JPG — when your phone saves photos in WebP but the portal needs JPEG
  • BMP to JPG — when you have a scanned document in BMP format
  • Any format to WebP — for smaller file sizes with good quality

All conversions happen instantly in your browser. The quality slider lets you control the output — higher quality means larger file size, lower quality means smaller files.
